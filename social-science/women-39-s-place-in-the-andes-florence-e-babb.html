In Women’s Place in the Andes Florence E. Babb draws on long-term anthropological research to reexamine the complex interworkings of gender, race, and indigeneity in Peru and beyond.  Interweaving five new analytical chapters with six previously published works that exemplify currents in feminist anthropological thought, Babb argues that decolonizing feminism and engaging more fully with interlocutors from the South will lead to a deeper understanding of the iconic Andean women who are subjects of both national pride and everyday scorn. Going further, this book’s novel approach sets forth a collaborative methodology for rethinking gender and race in the Americas.
